Frequently Asked Questions

Getting Started

Creating an account is simple! Click the "Register" button in the top navigation bar, fill in your details including your email, password, first and last name, institution, and position. Once you submit the form, you'll be redirected to the login page where you can sign in and start building your research profile immediately.
Required information includes: email, password, first name, last name, institution, and position. Optional fields include: title, department, bio, location, profile picture, research interests, social media links, achievements, and external roles. While optional, filling out these fields makes your profile more comprehensive and discoverable.

Profile Management

To edit your profile, click on "Profile" in the navigation menu, then click the "Edit Profile" button. You can update your personal information, institution, position, department, bio, location, and social media links. Don't forget to save your changes when you're done!
You can change your profile picture in two ways: 1) Go to your profile page and click the camera icon on your profile picture, or 2) Use the "Edit Profile" page and upload a new profile picture there. Supported formats include JPG, PNG, and GIF.
On your profile page, look for the "Research Interests" section. If you're the profile owner, you can click the edit icon to modify your research interests. Simply enter your interests separated by commas, and click save. These interests will be visible to other researchers and used in search functionality.
Update your profile whenever there are significant changes: when you publish new papers, receive awards or grants, change institutions, take on new roles, or shift your research focus. Keeping your profile current ensures other researchers can find accurate information about your work and interests.
You can manually type your institution name in the institution field. Make sure to use the full official name of your institution for consistency. If you move to a new institution, simply update your profile with the new information.
A strong profile includes: 1) A clear, professional profile picture, 2) A comprehensive bio highlighting your research focus and background, 3) Detailed research interests with specific keywords, 4) Up-to-date publications with complete information, 5) Achievements and awards, 6) External roles that showcase your involvement in the research community, and 7) Contact information and social media links. Complete profiles are more discoverable and appear more credible to potential collaborators.
Yes! You can show or hide certain sections on your profile. Look for the "Add/Delete Section" buttons on your profile page. You can toggle the visibility of sections like Papers and Research Interests. You can also drag and drop sections to reorder them on your profile page.
Go to "Edit Profile" and scroll down to the Social Media Links section. You can add URLs for LinkedIn, Twitter, Instagram, and GitHub. These links will appear on your profile page, making it easy for other researchers to connect with you on different platforms.

Achievements & External Roles

External Roles are positions you hold outside your primary institution, such as visiting professor positions, editorial board memberships, committee roles, or advisory positions. To add them, go to your profile and click the "External Roles" button. You can add multiple roles with details including role title, organization, start/end year, and description. You can also reorder them by dragging and dropping.
On your profile page, click the "Achievements" button. You can add various types of achievements including awards, grants, honors, certifications, and other recognitions. For each achievement, provide a title, type, year received, and optional description. You can reorder achievements by dragging them to emphasize your most important accomplishments.

Paper Management

Click on "Profile" and then the "Upload Paper" button. Fill in the paper details including title, authors, journal, publication year, DOI, URL, abstract, and keywords. You can also upload a PDF file of your paper. The system supports automatic keyword extraction from PDF files to make the process easier. Once submitted, your paper will appear on your profile and in search results.
Yes! Navigate to "My Papers" from your profile to see all your uploaded papers. From there, you can click on individual papers to view details, edit information, or delete them if needed. When editing, you can update all paper details and even replace the PDF file.
Currently, Research'D supports PDF files for paper uploads. Make sure your file is in PDF format before uploading. The system can also extract keywords automatically from your PDF to help populate the keywords field.
Yes! When uploading or editing a paper, use the "Authors" field to list all co-authors. Separate multiple authors with commas. Include yourself in the author list if you contributed to the paper.
When uploading or editing a paper, you can manually enter keywords in the keywords field, or use the automatic keyword extraction feature if you've uploaded a PDF. Keywords help other researchers find your papers through search. Separate multiple keywords with commas.
Yes! Add all papers you contributed to, regardless of your author position. Make sure to list all co-authors accurately in the authors field. This provides a complete picture of your research contributions and helps others find your collaborative work.
While direct integration with Google Scholar or ORCID is not currently available, you can include DOIs and URLs for your papers, which link to official publication sources. You can also add links to your Google Scholar profile or ORCID in your bio or social media links section.
When viewing a paper's details, if a PDF is available, you'll see a download or view button. Click it to open the paper in your browser. You can also use any DOI or URL links provided to access the paper through official publication channels.

Search & Discovery

Click on "Search" in the navigation menu. You can search by researcher name, institution, position, or research interests. Use the filter options on the left to narrow down results by institution, position, or specific research interests. You can also sort results by name, institution, or position.
On the Search page, switch to the "Papers" tab. You can search papers by title, authors, journal, abstract, or keywords. Filter results by journal name or publication year. Sort options include year, title, journal, or creation date to help you find the most relevant research.
Yes! On the Search page, select the "All" tab to search both researchers and papers simultaneously. This is useful when you're exploring a research topic and want to find both the people working in the field and their published work.
If you can't find a researcher, try different search terms: use their full name, try alternative spellings, search by their institution, or look for their published papers. The researcher may not have created an account yet, or they might be listed under a different name variation.
If your profile isn't showing up in searches, check that: 1) You've completed your basic profile information (name, institution, position), 2) Your research interests contain relevant keywords, 3) You've added content like papers or achievements. Newly created profiles should appear in search results immediately, but having more complete information improves visibility.
To increase your profile's visibility: 1) Complete all profile sections including bio, research interests, and institution, 2) Upload your research papers with detailed abstracts and keywords, 3) Add achievements and external roles, 4) Include social media links, and 5) Keep your information up-to-date. Profiles with more complete information appear more prominently in search results.

Collaboration & Networking

You can find researchers' contact information on their profile pages. Many researchers include their email addresses, social media links (LinkedIn, Twitter, GitHub), and institutional affiliations. Use these channels to reach out for collaboration or networking opportunities.
Yes! You can view any researcher's profile by clicking on their name in search results or paper listings. Their public profile will show their bio, research interests, publications, achievements, external roles, and contact information if they've chosen to share it.

Account Management

Password management features are currently being developed. In the meantime, please keep your current password secure and use a strong, unique password for your account.
Password recovery features are currently being developed. Make sure to remember your password or store it securely. We recommend using a password manager to keep track of your login credentials.
Each user should maintain only one Research'D account. If you need to update your information (such as changing institutions or positions), simply edit your existing profile rather than creating a new account. This helps maintain profile continuity and prevents confusion.
Account deletion features are currently being developed. If you urgently need to delete your account, you can remove all personal information from your profile as a temporary measure. Be aware that deleting your account will remove all your profile information, papers, and associated data permanently.
Data export features are being developed. In the meantime, you can manually copy your profile information and download any papers you've uploaded directly from your profile and papers pages.

Privacy & Security

Research'D takes your privacy and security seriously. Your password is securely hashed and stored. Profile information you choose to share is visible to other users to facilitate academic networking, but you control what information you add. For more details, please review our Privacy Policy.
Yes! All current features of Research'D are completely free to use. This includes account creation, profile management, paper uploads, search functionality, and all networking features.

Technical Information

Research'D works best on modern browsers including Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Safari, and Microsoft Edge. Make sure your browser is updated to the latest version for the best experience. Most features also work on mobile browsers.
Yes! Research'D is fully responsive and works on mobile phones and tablets. You can browse profiles, search for researchers and papers, and even upload content from your mobile device. The interface automatically adapts to your screen size.
Click the moon/sun icon in the navigation bar to toggle between light and dark themes. Your preference will be saved and applied automatically when you return to the site.

Troubleshooting

If you find a duplicate profile of yourself, you can simply use one profile and leave the other inactive. Make sure your primary profile is complete and up-to-date. For incorrect information on other profiles, remember that users are responsible for maintaining their own profile information.
If you're having trouble with uploaded files: 1) Ensure the file is in PDF format and not corrupted, 2) Check that the file size isn't too large (try compressing large files), 3) Try uploading again with a fresh copy of the file, 4) Clear your browser cache and cookies, then try again. If problems persist, try using a different browser.
If you experience technical issues: 1) Try refreshing the page (Ctrl+R or Cmd+R), 2) Clear your browser cache and cookies, 3) Try a different browser, 4) Check your internet connection, 5) Ensure your browser is up-to-date. Most issues can be resolved with these basic troubleshooting steps.
